# Open Dental

**Last enriched**: 2025-11-28.

## Overview

Open-source dental practice management software. Self-hosted or via optional cloud partners. US-founded (2003), small-team operation. [CONFIRMED — opendental.com]

AU/NZ presence is entirely grassroots — estimated ~50 AU practices using it. [INFERENCE — triangulated from AU dental forums + a single Reddit thread]

## Product

- Full PMS — charting, scheduling, billing, imaging. [CONFIRMED]
- Feature-rich by open-source standards. Actively maintained. [CONFIRMED]
- UX is utilitarian / dated. Not friendly to non-technical users. [CONFIRMED]
- Recall: basic, built-in. Same limitations as commercial competitors. [CONFIRMED]

## Pricing

- Software: free (open source).
- Support contract: $149 USD/mo for direct support.
- Cloud hosting via third-party partners: varies, typically $100-200 USD/mo. [CONFIRMED]

## GTM

- No GTM in any traditional sense. Pure inbound + word-of-mouth + dev-community channels. [CONFIRMED]
- Active forum and user community (self-help primary channel). [CONFIRMED]

## Team & funding

- Founder-led small team (~15 people per LinkedIn). [CONFIRMED]
- Bootstrapped, no funding. [CONFIRMED]

## Moats

- **Open-source network effects**: community contributions, customisation ecosystem. [CONFIRMED]
- **Price**: free is hard to beat. [CONFIRMED]
- **Longevity**: 20+ years of stability. [CONFIRMED]

## Threats (to DentaSync)

- **LOW**: Open Dental's addressable user segment is technically competent dental practices willing to self-host or pay for a 3rd-party hoster. This is a narrow slice of the market, and not overlapping with our ICP (non-technical 2-4 chair independents).

## Where they're weak

- **Technical barrier to entry**: most independent dentists will never self-host. [CONFIRMED]
- **No AI, no modern UX, no automation layer**: we'd be adding what they lack. [CONFIRMED]
- **Support model**: community-first; for a dentist who wants 24h support, Open Dental is a non-starter. [INFERENCE]

## Strategic implications for DentaSync

Open Dental is interesting not as a competitor but as a **potential integration target**. If we build the recall-only tool, adding Open Dental to our integration stack is cheap (open schema) and picks up the ~50 AU technical practices. Not a priority, but a free tactical win.
