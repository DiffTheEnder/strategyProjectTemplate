# Context Snapshot — ANZ Dental Market

**Generated**: 2025-12-05. Refreshed monthly unless a major signal lands.

---

## Sizing headlines

- **Total ANZ dental practices**: ~11,500 (8,500 AU + 3,000 NZ)
- **SAM (2-8 chair independents)**: ~6,500 practices
- **TAM (annual PMS + add-on spend, SAM only)**: **$94M AUD ARR**
- **SOM year 3 (at $400/mo recall-only, 4% penetration)**: **$1.56M ARR**

Evidence: IBISWorld 2024, ADA Practice Income Survey 2023, NZ Ministry of Health 2024 — triangulated against 3 competitor pricing pages.

## Dynamics (one line each)

- Consolidation: DSO/corporate now ~11% of ANZ, +2%/yr — out of scope.
- Legacy PMS lock-in: Dentrix, Oasis, Praktika, D4W. Switching is painful → add-on wins over replacement.
- ANZ compliance (Medicare CDBS, HICAPS e-claim): real moat for ANZ-native tools.
- Tech fatigue: dentists 45+ willing to pay for "one less thing".
- Post-COVID recall backlog: 3-5 years of deferred maintenance patients — tailwind.
- PE-backed incumbents (Henry Schein, Dentrix) slowing innovation — tailwind.

## White space

1. ANZ-native compliance (CDBS, HICAPS) — no US competitor handles well
2. Add-on positioning (layer on top of Dentrix/Oasis) — all competitors are full PMS plays
3. Recall-only at $300-$500/mo — below all PMS pricing, above standalone recall tools
